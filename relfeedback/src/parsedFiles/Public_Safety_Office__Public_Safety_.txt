        &nbsp; &nbsp;When to Call 911?​
POLICE&nbsp;- If you should see someone committing a crime, a person who is about to commit a crime, or a crime has already occurred.
FIRE&nbsp;- On all actual fires, smell of smoke or gas, or if you should see smoke rising from an object that is on fire.
MEDICAL&nbsp;- If a person should need immediate medical attention.
&nbsp; &nbsp;What to do when you call 911?​
	Give the&nbsp;location of the emergency.
	State what&nbsp;type of emergency&nbsp;that you have.
	Give the dispatcher&nbsp;your name.
	Give the dispatcher&nbsp;your phone number&nbsp;and what location you are calling from if it differs from the location of the emergency.
Non-Emergency Telephone Numbers&nbsp;If you have a non-emergency situation, call 864-5900 or 864-5913. If you would like to report a crime anonymously, call KU&nbsp;CRIMESTOPPERS&nbsp;at 864-8888. You can anonymously provide information that may lead to the arrest of suspects who have committed a crime.
