        ContainerOverviewPrinting, copying and scanning services for students, faculty, staff and campus visitors are available in&nbsp;many central campus locations&nbsp;such as computing labs and libraries via your KU Online ID.
See&nbsp;Campus Printing &amp; Copying Locations&nbsp;for a list of computer labs that have multifunction devices (MFD) that can print, copy and scan on one device. KU uses a tracking program connected to the KU Card system to identify users and track printing costs.&nbsp;

Printing for Students
Students can print from computers in open computing labs and all library&nbsp;locations across the KU campus (see locations) using their KU Online ID. You also need available funds in your Beak 'Em Bucks account on your KU Card. When you log into a computer in these open locations, you will see the amount of free printing or Beak 'Em Bucks funds available to you. Be sure to log off the computers when you are done, so that others do not use your account to print.

What is Express Printing?
Express or Quick Print workstations allow users to print quickly from a USB drive, email attachment or web storage without logging in to the computer. Users must provide a KU Online ID and password when prompted, and their KU card must have sufficient funds to print. Usage of the Express Print station is limited to a maximum of five minutes per session. See Print, Copy &amp; Scan Locations for a list of locations that have Express Printing workstations.

Cost:&nbsp;Printing or copying costs $.08 per page for black and white and $0.48 for color. You can pay with funds you add to your  KU Beak 'Em Bucks account  or by purchasing printing credits ($1.00 minimum).&nbsp;Visit&nbsp;Beak 'Em Bucks Overview&nbsp;to learn how to add funds to your KU Card "Beak 'Em Bucks" account.
Checking Your Printing &amp; Copying Balance:
To check your printing and copying balance, login to myprint.ku.edu&nbsp;with your KU Online ID.&nbsp;
Visitors:
See "Printing for Visitors to Campus" above for information on purchasing printing credits for visitors, including purchasing locations.
AccessPrinting
Anyone with a KU Online ID can log in and print from computers in open computing labs and all library&nbsp;locations across the KU campus. You will also need to have access to&nbsp;free printing&nbsp;(students only) or available funds in your  KU Card "Beak 'Em Bucks" account. When you log into a campus lab computer, you will see the amount of free printing or KU Card "Beak 'Em Bucks" funds available to you. Be sure to log off the computers when you are done, so that others do not use your account to print.&nbsp;
Copying
Copying options include both black and white and color copying, with optional duplexing. Copying costs are the same as printing: $0.08 per page for black and white and $0.48 for color. If you have a KU Online ID, you will enter that and your password at the device using the touch screen and keyboard. Campus users will see their available printing/copying balance through the touch screen, immediately after authentication. Visitors who have purchased printing credits will enter the user name and password on the visitor access form they purchased.
Step-by-step instructions
	On the touch screen, press "Username." Type your KU Online ID (or visitor access user name) using the attached keyboard, and press "Enter."
	Press "Password." Type your password using the attached keyboard, and press "Enter."
	Press "OK."
	The screen will display your current copying/printing balances. Press "OK."
	The touch screen now has all of the options and functions of a copier. Use the keypad to enter the number of copies, adjust color, size, and magnification.
	Once you are finished, press the "Access" key on the keypad to log off of the device.
Note: The device will automatically log off after 60 seconds of inaction.
Scanning
Scanning options via multifunction devices (MFD) include:
	Scan to email&nbsp;- Scan print documents and email them to yourself. View&nbsp;video instructions&nbsp;or&nbsp;Knowledge Base article.
	Scan to USB&nbsp;- Scan print documents to a USB drive. View&nbsp;video instructions&nbsp;or&nbsp;Knowledge Base article.
